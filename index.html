Autonomy Lab / SFU Mountain Dataset


A dataset of woodland trail navigation on a ground-based robot in changing environmental conditions.


We apologize, but we are still evaluating solutions for hosting the SFU Mountain Dataset in its unabridged form. The full dataset consists of several hundred GB of sensor data including full video from 6 cameras, and range data from 2 laser scanners, plus calibration parameters for each sensor. We are working to provide the data in the form of ROS bag files, compressed image files, and CSV text files.

In the mean time, please feel free to download our ground-truth place matches: frames from each camera at locations separated by approximately 10 meters, hand-matched between dry, wet, dusk, and night traversals. Here are some example place matches for each camera across all four sessions:

Raw Sensor Data

The dataset in its purest form is available as ROS bag files recorded directly from the robot. These are the original recordings from which all other formats were exported; we modified them only to synchronize sensor timestamps.

ROS Bag Files

dry dry-a.bag (checksum) dry-b.bag (checksum)
wet wet-a.bag (checksum) wet-b.bag (checksum)
dusk dusk-a.bag (checksum) dusk-b.bag (checksum)
night night-a.bag (checksum) night-b.bag (checksum)

In addition to the ROS bag files, we provide non-camera sensor data in CSV text files in gzipped .tgz format. Calibration parameters for each sensor are included in calibration.yaml if applicable.

JPEG images from the cameras are provided at full 30 Hz, and also at subsampled rates of 3 Hz and 0.3 Hz for convenience.
